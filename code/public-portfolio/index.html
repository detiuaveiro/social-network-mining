Get a feel for the flow of information!

Minerva is a tool for analyzing the connections observable on the Twitter social network. It allows you to study the connections between people, how the information gets passed around between users, how news and media get propagated throughout and infiltrate Twitter's "deep web" of private user circles.

available as a Web App

How It Works

Tweak Bots

Define our Bots' behaviours by creating policies to govern their behaviour. Prioritize certain tags, categories or users to have specified bots that focus on the content that interests you.

Bots Gather Information

Our Bot's independently scour Twitter in search of meaningful content. They attempt to follow users of interest, reply to tweets and showcase human behaviour in order to gather data.

Analyze Results

Analyze data recieved in real time through an extensive amount of graphs and tables that show the influx of Tweets, Bot Activity, Network Graph, amongst other statistics.

Awesome Features

Human-like Bots

Our Bots implement a lot of high-level Machine Learning models and Artificial Intelligence algorithms to mimic Human Behaviour and have prooven to be able to pass the Turing Test. They're independent and constantly scour Twitter as a normal user would.

Easy to use

Our web-app showcases a clean and user-friendly design and features, without jeopardizing extensibility. Our interface is easy to navigate and offers high-level abstractions that allow anyone to query our network graph and visualize all data gathered.

Customizable Reports

Generate tweakable reports by querying our database utilizing our high-level abstraction interface. Specify the information you're looking for and receive a report in either CSV or JSON format for posteriour analysis.

Big Data

Explore the hundereds of thousands of data our Bots have (and continue to) collect. Analyze a ludicrous amount of Twitter Users' informations, such as their profile info, tweets, replies, quotes, retweets, follower count, amongst others! Read up on the latest tweets added to our database or check what images and videos users are publishing.

Network Visualization

Visualize and go through the network that results from all of our Bots' hard work. See the connections between users and other users or even with tweets, manipulate our physics-enabled network graph, focus and search for nodes or make more advanced queries to find the sub-network you want to see.

Policy Definition

Define customizeable policies to fine-tweak our Bots' behaviours. Make them focus on certain types of content defined by tags or make them attempt to follow certain users. These can be assigned to each bot individually, created at will and even edited. Just assign them to a bot and sit back while our advanced Machine Learning Model trains our Bots.

How We Made It

Our Backend

Our backend was entirely written in Python3 due to it's extensible features and because it was the language most familiar to our development team. As for our REST API, which is used as a middle point between our Web App and our databases, we chose to make it in Django.

Our Databases

As our problem is one that fits in the paradigm of Big Data, we had to choose our DBMS' carefully. We opted to go with Neo4j to store our entities and the connections between them since, as a Graph Database, it fit the model pretty well. This DBMS, however, isn't very well optimized to treat loads of informaton, and as such we chose to store each of our entities' properties in a MongoDB database. It's imperative that these two systems are consistent between each other. PostgreSQL was used to store statistical data for further analysis and inclusion in graphs and finally Redis was used to implement a Caching system in order to decrease the load time of certain heavy-weight queries. An additional MongoDB database was also created further down the line to store our Machine Learning models, which are used to train and govern our Bots' policies.

Our Message Brokers

Our product is made up of several services that communicate between each other. As such we had to make use of mechanisms that would allow for such interactions to take place. For most of our communications we utilized RabbitMQ, which permits the swapping of messages even if services are currently unavailable (although with the promise that the message will eventually reach it's destined service). We also used ZeroMQ for the communication between our Control Centre and Response Centre due to it's ease of usage and light-weightedness (and since ParlAI already possesses a server that integrates well with connections made using this tool).

Our Bot's Intelligence

In order to achieve a good performance in terms of mimicry of human behaviour, we had to implement several Artifial Intelligence paradigms to regulate the Bots' behaviour. As the project evolved so did our AI and ML algorithms and as such we had to utilize different tool, libraries and frameworks such as Keras ParlAI, NLTK and skLearn. For our Deep Learning algorithms we built a Convolutional Neural Network using Keras. This was done to treat the problem of Text Classification used by the Follow Centre service (which deals with the decision of following a given user in accordance to the Bots' policies). The model's training processes were then made faster by using the skLearn library which allows for the tunning of the model's parameters in order to implement Parallel Computing, hence taking advanced of the Multi-Core processors present in our servers. Other AI algorithms were used in order to allow our Bots to generate human-like responses to tweets. These problems, related to natural-language interpretation, were solved, first using ELIZA style AI. This, however, prooved to generate repetitive responses, and as such we turned to Facebook's Open Source tool - ParlAI. This, mixed in with a Poly-Encoder that we re-trained using our own tweets dataset, managed to improve our Bots' response quality by a visible margin.

Our Frontend

Minerva's web app was built using React JS and consists on a very user-friendly and clean dashboard that allows for the easy visualization of our gathered data in graphs, tables and exportable reports, alongside the ability to define and control our Bot's policies. For our graphs we utilized the ReChart library and for our Network Graph we used React Vis.

How the Bots Work

Passing the Turing Test

Our Bots are intelligent and capable of mimicking human behaviour. So intelligent, in fact, that they've even, inadvertadly, passed the Turing Test. They're capable of replying to real users' tweets by creating retweets and participating in threads of conversation, which leads to direct interaction with actual human beings. They're behaviour is governed by three microservices which are vital to our project. Each of these places a unique piece in the complex puzzle that are our bots AI.

Control Centre

In our project, we had to make sure that the bots themselves didn't have too much logic in them and were as light-weight as possible. As such, they won't connect to our databases or make decisions on their own. Instead they will contact our Control Centre; this is the entity that will serve as the middle man between the databases and the decision making entities and our bots. It's very important that this module is ready to receive thousands of messages from various bots, and effectively wrap the data it received to save the data in our DBs and send the Decision Centres.

Response Centre

In order to imitate human behaviour to the furthest extent possible, we implemented a way to respond to interactions (i.e tweets) using a machine learning model. At first we started by using an old but widely popular response model based around ELIZA. However, teh responses generated were very repetitive so we opted to go another route. We used the ParlAI Open Source tool with a pre-trained language interpreter and response generator and a Poly-encoder which we trained using tweets we gathered over time. All of these are submodules of our Response Centre service, which the Control Centre connects upon allowing a bot to respond to a tweet sending it the tweet and a set of policies that control the bots behaviour. This centre then responds with an appropriate message for the bot to respond with.

Follow Centre

One of our Bots' main directives is to follow users. Due to the importance of this feature we decided it'd be best to implement a service capable of discriminating which users to follow in accordance to the policies that govern each Bots' behaviour. For each policy we train a Deep Learning Model using previously obtained tweets, which are constantly updated, that are marked with the tags that define said policy. When a decision to follow or ignore a user has to be made this module takes as inputs the description and most recent tweets of the targeted user (if they're not a private account). Besides descriminating profiles, this service is also capable of regulating actions related to the maintenance and consistency of data used in our models, detect changes made to our previously trained policies (so that they may be re-trained) and add/eliminate new models in accordance to the creation of deletion of policies. All messages interchange between this service and our Bots passes through the Control Centre so from a Bot's point of view they need only ask the Control Centre. This centre then sends the request to our Follows Center.

The Web App

View User Info

You can search for all of the users that are in our database by name or twitter tag and even filter to show protected accounts only! Whilst inside a user's profile you can access a plethora of information about them such as their tweets, retweets and quotes, number of followers, just to name a few. You can also access the people their following or that are getting followed by's profile!

Analyze Graphs

Study a diverse set of statistics with easy to read and easy to use graphs. Get information on how our bots are doing by checking the growth of the cumulative or new activity, entities or relationships charts to see how our databases grows in real time!

Travel the Network

Have fun messing around with our physics enabled network graph and get a feel for how users relate between each other and tweets. Create advanced queries with our easy to use tool that abstracts Neo4js Cypher language which allows for anyone, even the less tech-savy, to draw the subgraph they wish for!

Generate Reports

Generate extensive CSV and JSON reports for further analysis. With our Report Generation tool you can query our databases and generate downloadable files that contain just the data you wish to study!

Check out the Bots

See each Bot's individual profile and check how their performance has been. View the Bot's followers, the users he's following, their tweets, their activity and directly control them by assigning them a new policy or posting a tweet using their account.

Define Policies

Control our bots' behaviour and have them specialize in finding information of certain types of content. Each bot can be assigned a different set of policies that either make them look for certain types of tweets or make them attempt to track down and follow certain users!
