Social Network Mining

Get a feel for the flow of information!

Minerva is a tool for analyzing the connections observable on the Twitter social network. It allows you to study the connections between people, how the information gets passed around between users, how news and media get propagated throughout and infiltrate Twitter's "deep web" of private user circles.

available as a Web App

How It Works

Tweak Bots

Define our Bots' behaviours by creating policies to govern their behaviour. Prioritize certain tags, categories or users to have specified bots that focus on the content that interests you.

Bots Gather Information

Our Bot's independently scour Twitter in search of meaningful content. They attempt to follow users of interest, reply to tweets and showcase human behaviour in order to gather data.

Analyze Results

Analyze data recieved in real time through an extensive amount of graphs and tables that show the influx of Tweets, Bot Activity, Network Graph, amongst other statistics.

Awesome Features

Human-like Bots

Our Bots implement a lot of high-level Machine Learning models and Artificial Intelligence algorithms to mimic Human Behaviour and have prooven to be able to pass the Turing Test. They're independent and constantly scour Twitter as a normal user would.

Easy to use

Our web-app showcases a clean and user-friendly design and features, without jeopardizing extensibility. Our interface is easy to navigate and offers high-level abstractions that allow anyone to query our network graph and visualize all data gathered.

Customizable Reports

Generate tweakable reports by querying our database utilizing our high-level abstraction interface. Specify the information you're looking for and receive a report in either CSV or JSON format for posteriour analysis.

Big Data

Explore the hundereds of thousands of data our Bots have (and continue to) collect. Analyze a ludicrous amount of Twitter Users' informations, such as their profile info, tweets, replies, quotes, retweets, follower count, amongst others! Read up on the latest tweets added to our database or check what images and videos users are publishing.

Network Visualization

Visualize and go through the network that results from all of our Bots' hard work. See the connections between users and other users or even with tweets, manipulate our physics-enabled network graph, focus and search for nodes or make more advanced queries to find the sub-network you want to see.

Policy Definition

Define customizeable policies to fine-tweak our Bots' behaviours. Make them focus on certain types of content defined by tags or make them attempt to follow certain users. These can be assigned to each bot individually, created at will and even edited. Just assign them to a bot and sit back while our advanced Machine Learning Model trains our Bots.

How We Made It

Our Backend

Our backend was entirely written in Python3 due to it's extensible features and because it was the language most familiar to our development team. As for our REST API, which is used as a middle point between our Web App and our databases, we chose to make it in Django.

Our Databases

As our problem is one that fits in the paradigm of Big Data, we had to choose our DBMS' carefully. We opted to go with Neo4j to store our entities and the connections between them since, as a Graph Database, it fit the model pretty well. This DBMS, however, isn't very well optimized to treat loads of informaton, and as such we chose to store each of our entities' properties in a MongoDB database. It's imperative that these two systems are consistent between each other. PostgreSQL was used to store statistical data for further analysis and inclusion in graphs and finally Redis was used to implement a Caching system in order to decrease the load time of certain heavy-weight queries. An additional MongoDB database was also created further down the line to store our Machine Learning models, which are used to train and govern our Bots' policies.
